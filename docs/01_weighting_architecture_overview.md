## üß† High-Level Summary of CI

The Corruptibility Index (CI) is a multi-metric, evidence-based scoring engine designed to evaluate corruption risk using:

- financial anomalies  
- donor influence  
- real-estate patterning  
- lobbying entanglements  
- transactional appointments  
- behavioral patterns  
- legal irregularities  
- investigative pressure signals  

CI produces a deterministic score from **0 to 100**, supported by:

- strict normalization  
- metric-specific formulas  
- outlier control  
- temporal decay  
- a confidence model integrating source reliability  
- reproducible AI extraction rules  

The CI-Core pipeline ensures **auditability, reproducibility, and defensibility**.
